# Training FAQ

## Is failure required for hypertrophy?

No. Useful stimulus can be achieved without failure, especially with moderate to heavy loads. Being too far from failure is usually the bigger problem.

## Is light weight useless for growth?

No. Light loads can work if sets are hard enough, though they are often more uncomfortable and less time-efficient.

## Is youth strength training dangerous?

Not when properly supervised and progressed. Major professional bodies support it.

## Is soreness a sign of progress?

Not reliably. Soreness is a signal of novelty or tissue stress, not a direct growth metric.

## Should everyone deload every fourth week?

No. Deloads are better triggered by fatigue and performance context than by superstition.
