# Training Knowledge Graph

## Core Relations

- Functional anatomy -> constrains biomechanics.
- Biomechanics -> shapes exercise selection, technique, and joint loading.
- Exercise physiology -> explains acute fatigue and chronic adaptation.
- Energy systems -> explain time-domain specificity in CrossFit, conditioning, and interval work.
- Hypertrophy <- influenced by volume, effort proximity, exercise selection, and recovery.
- Max strength <- influenced by neural adaptation, specificity, motor skill, load, and fatigue management.
- Power <- depends on strength base, velocity exposure, and technical efficiency.
- Muscular endurance <- depends on local metabolic tolerance, task specificity, and pacing.
- Cardiovascular endurance <- depends on central and peripheral adaptations plus modality specificity.
- Mobility and flexibility -> affect position access, movement options, and tolerance, but do not alone guarantee skill or injury reduction.
- Sleep + nutrition + recovery -> modulate readiness, adaptation, and repeatability of training stress.
- Periodization -> organizes stress over time.
- Autoregulation -> adjusts day-level loading to actual readiness.
- RPE/RIR/VBT -> operational tools for autoregulation.
- Functional overreaching -> can be useful if recovery is planned.
- Non-functional overreaching and overtraining -> performance costs exceed useful adaptation.
- Deload -> reduces fatigue and restores capacity for productive overload.
- Tendon and ligament health -> influenced by loading magnitude, rate, chronic progression, and recovery.
- Youth training -> emphasizes technique, exposure, long-term development, and supervision.
- Elite training -> emphasizes marginal gains, sport specificity, monitoring, and constraint management.

## Domain Relations

- CrossFit combines weightlifting, gymnastics, power, conditioning, and pacing under fatigue.
- Weightlifting emphasizes force production and technique at high velocity.
- Powerlifting emphasizes maximal force in squat, bench press, and deadlift with strict competitive standards.
- Sports nutrition supports body composition, training quality, performance, and recovery.

## Evidence Relations

- ACSM position stands -> broad adult guidance.
- NSCA position statements -> applied S&C and youth development guidance.
- Meta-analyses by Schoenfeld, Krieger, Grgic, and others -> high-value synthesis for hypertrophy, load, frequency, and effort.
