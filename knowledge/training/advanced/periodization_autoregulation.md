# Periodization, Autoregulation, RPE, RIR, VBT, Overreaching, Overtraining, and Deload

## Executive Summary

Periodization organizes training across time. Autoregulation adjusts day-to-day decisions based on actual readiness and performance. The strongest practical approach for many lifters is planned structure plus autoregulated execution.

## Complete Explanation

### Fundamental Concepts

- Periodization: structured sequencing of volume, intensity, specificity, and recovery.
- Autoregulation: dynamic adjustment based on feedback.
- RPE and RIR: effort anchors.
- VBT: velocity used as an objective signal for load prescription and fatigue management.

### Intermediate Concepts

- Fixed percentage programs work best when readiness is stable and 1RM estimates are current.
- RPE/RIR work well when athletes are educated and consistent.
- VBT can improve precision, but device quality and interpretation matter.
- Functional overreaching can be productive when followed by recovery.

### Advanced Concepts

- Overtraining syndrome is rare compared with ordinary fatigue mismanagement.
- Non-functional overreaching is more common and more practically relevant.
- Deloads are tools, not rituals; they are useful when fatigue, motivation, pain, or performance indicate diminishing returns.

## Best Practices

- Use block priorities instead of trying to maximize every quality at once.
- Employ autoregulation especially for advanced lifters, high-stress life periods, and technical sports.
- Use deloads when performance and recovery markers justify them.

## Common Errors

- Turning RPE into guesswork with no calibration.
- Treating every hard block as overtraining.
- Using VBT numbers without understanding exercise-specific velocity profiles.

## Scientific Divergences

- RPE/RIR vs percentage-based loading: evidence generally supports autoregulated methods as at least viable and often advantageous in fluctuating readiness contexts, but exact superiority depends on athlete level and implementation.
- VBT: useful especially in strength-power contexts, but not a universal replacement for coaching judgment.
