# CrossFit, Weightlifting, and Powerlifting

## Executive Summary

These domains overlap in barbell literacy but diverge in adaptation targets. CrossFit rewards broad work capacity under task variance. Weightlifting rewards explosive technical precision. Powerlifting rewards maximal force expression in three lifts under strict standards.

## Complete Explanation

### CrossFit

- Demands: mixed-modal capacity, skill breadth, pacing, transitions, gymnastics competence, barbell cycling, and fatigue tolerance.
- Common limiter: too much random intensity, too little progressive skill and strength planning.

### Weightlifting

- Demands: snatch and clean and jerk technique, positional strength, speed under the bar, pull quality, receiving positions, and fatigue discipline.
- Common limiter: chasing heavy singles without enough technical volume or mobility access.

### Powerlifting

- Demands: squat, bench, deadlift specificity; bracing; setup repeatability; and fatigue management.
- Common limiter: too much accessory chaos or too little specificity near competition.

## Best Practices

- CrossFit athletes need true strength phases and aerobic development, not only metcons.
- Weightlifters need technical exposures while relatively fresh.
- Powerlifters need enough practice in competition standards.

## Errors

- CrossFit: mistaking exhaustion for progress.
- Weightlifting: adding load faster than technical control.
- Powerlifting: peaking too often and accumulating joint irritation.
