# Program Design Examples

## Executive Summary

These examples translate evidence into decision patterns. They are not templates to copy blindly; they are scaffolds.

## Example 1: Hypertrophy-Oriented Intermediate Lifter

- 4 days per week.
- Each muscle 2x weekly.
- Most work at 0-3 RIR.
- Volume progressed only when performance and recovery are stable.

## Example 2: Powerlifting Strength Block

- Competition squat, bench, deadlift anchored weekly.
- Secondary variations for weak points.
- Longer rest, tighter technical standards.
- Deload when bar speed, joint tolerance, or readiness trends deteriorate.

## Example 3: CrossFit Athlete with Weak Strength Base

- Two dedicated strength sessions.
- Two engine sessions.
- Two mixed-modal sport sessions.
- One low-stress technical day or full rest.

## Example 4: Adolescent Athlete

- Supervised full-body sessions 2-3x weekly.
- Focus on movement literacy, jumps, landings, bracing, and gradual loading.

## Example 5: Return After Tendon Pain

- Rebuild load with controlled pain tolerance framework.
- Reintroduce stretch-shortening and sport specificity only after basic loading tolerance returns.
