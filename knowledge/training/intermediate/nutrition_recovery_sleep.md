# Sports Nutrition, Recovery, and Sleep

## Executive Summary

Nutrition and recovery do not replace training quality, but poor nutrition and poor sleep will cap adaptation. Protein adequacy, energy availability, carbohydrate timing relative to workload, hydration, and sufficient sleep are the most robust levers.

## Complete Explanation

### Fundamental Concepts

- Recovery is restoration plus adaptation.
- Energy availability affects endocrine status, recovery quality, and training readiness.
- Protein supports muscle protein synthesis.
- Carbohydrates matter more as training volume and glycolytic demand rise.
- Sleep supports cognition, reaction time, recovery, and adaptation.

### Intermediate Concepts

- Hypertrophy phases need enough energy and protein to sustain hard volume.
- Strength phases still benefit from carbohydrate support even when total volume is lower.
- Mixed-modal and CrossFit athletes often require more carbohydrate emphasis than physique-focused low-output plans assume.

### Advanced Concepts

- Body composition phases should trade off adaptation speed against fatigue and performance cost.
- Low energy availability can quietly degrade adaptation, mood, libido, sleep, and menstrual function.
- Sleep debt compounds decision quality and pain sensitivity.

## Best Practices

- Keep daily protein adequate and distributed.
- Match carbohydrate intake to workload, especially before and after hard sessions.
- Protect sleep duration and regularity first before adding supplements.
- Use caffeine strategically, not constantly.

## Common Errors

- Cutting calories too aggressively during heavy training blocks.
- Using supplements to patch poor sleep and poor food habits.
- Treating hydration as optional in hot or high-volume work.

## Practical Applications

- Hypertrophy mesocycle: stable surplus or small surplus, adequate protein, high training quality.
- Cutting phase: preserve intensity, reduce volume as needed, keep protein high, manage fatigue aggressively.
- Competition week: reduce novelty, prioritize digestion, hydration, and sleep routine.
